Food Waste's Impact:

  • 1.3 billion tons of food are wasted each year.

    • That's enough food to feed all the hungry people in the world.

    • 30% - 40% of all food in the United States is thrown out.

    • In the United States alone, $161 Billion of food are thrown out.
  • 45 trillion gallons of water are wasted each year.

    • That's enough water to fill 70 million olympic sized swimming pools.

    • Or enough water to fill up over 160,000 Empire State Buildings.

    • In other words, a quarter of the Pacific Ocean is wasted growing food that is thrown away.
  • 350 million barrels of oil are wasted each year.

    • That's the same amount of oil as the United States currently has in its reserves.

    • This is enough energy to fuel a traditional lightbulb for 27 billion years.
  • 3.4 million acres of land are used up growing food no one is going to eat.

    • That's about the size of the United States, India, and Colombia combined.

    • You could fit around 2.6 million football fields in the land used to grow wasted food.

    • You could fit 680,000 people on this land, which would be a major step in combating overpopulation.
  • 170 million metric tons of GHG are released in the decomposition of food alone.

    • This accounts for around 6% of all GHG emissions in the world and about a quarter of GHG emissions from food
    • That is roughly equivalent to 20 billion gallons of gasoline.

    • That's enough fuel to drive for 436 billion miles or enough gas to drive around the circumference of the Earth 17 million times.

What Can You Do?

Track Food Inventory

One way to lower food waste is to collect food from restaurants or supermarkets that throw stuff away after each day. This is done by companies such as City Harvest or Dreaming Out Loud. More companies can be found here.

Meal Plannning

Another way is to plan out what you are going to buy each week using MyPlate to make healthy meals. By only buying what you need, one saves on wasted food.

Composting

One can also learn to compost through websites like this one. This way any food that isn't eaten from banana peels to egg shells can be reused to help lower food waste
